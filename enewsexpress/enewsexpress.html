E-news Express A/B Testing¶

The design team of the E-news Express, an online news portal, has researched and created a new landing page that has a new outline & more relevant content shown compared to the old page. In order to test the effectiveness of the new landing page in gathering new subscribers, the Data Science team conducted an experiment by randomly selecting 100 users and dividing them equally into two groups. The existing landing page was served to the first group (control group) and the new landing page to the second group (treatment group). Data regarding the interaction of users in both groups with the two versions of the landing page was collected.

Null and Alternate hypothesis¶

Let $\mu_1$ and $\mu_2$ be the mean times spent on the new and old landing pages respectively.

$H_0: \mu_1 = \mu_2$ (The mean time spent on the new landing page and the old landing page are the same)

$H_a: \mu_1 > \mu_2$ (The mean time spent on the new landing page is greater than the mean time spent on the old landing page)

Test selection¶

In [213]:
print("The std dev of treatment group time:", data_newpage['time_spent_on_the_page'].std())
print("The std dev of control group time:", data_oldpage['time_spent_on_the_page'].std())

The std dev of treatment group time: 1.8170310387878263
The std dev of control group time: 2.581974849306046
  • The test is concerned with analysis run on only one variable time_spent_on_the_page - Univariate analysis
  • The test is concerned with 2 independent samples
  • The times are measured on a continuous scale - continuous distribution
  • The sample size is 50 (>=30) and hence we can use the Central Limit Theorem's normal distribution for the sample means
  • The population variance is unknown - t-test
  • The problem statement indicates 'random selection of users' - random sampling
  • This is a check for equality of means
  • The std devs are unequal and not close to each other

Hence the most appropriate statistical test is independent 2-sample t-test with unequal std dev

p-value calculation and comparison with level of significance(α)¶

In [214]:
from scipy.stats import ttest_ind

stat, pvalue = ttest_ind(data_newpage['time_spent_on_the_page'], data_oldpage['time_spent_on_the_page'], equal_var=False, alternative='greater')
print('p-value = ', pvalue)

p-value =  0.0001392381225166549

The p-value is < (α=0.05)

Inference¶

  • Due to the above, there is enough statistical evidence to reject the null hypothesis.
  • Hence at a 5% level of significance, it is inferred that users spend more time on the new landing page (treatment group) than the old landing page (control group)

Null and Alternate hypothesis¶

Let $p_1$ and $p_2$ be the proportion of converted users on the new and old landing pages repectively

$H_0: p_1 = p_2$ (proportion of converted users on the new landing page and the old landing page are the same)

$H_a: p_1 > p_2$ (proportion of converted users on the new landing page is greater than proportion of converted users on the old landing page)

Test selection¶

  • The test is concerned with analysis run on one variable 'converted' - Univariate analysis
  • The test is concerned with 2 independent samples
  • The 'converted' variable can take only 2 states (Y or N) - binomial distribution
  • The problem statement indicates 'random selection of users' - random sampling
  • This is a check for proportions

Null and Alternate hypothesis¶

Let μ1, μ2, μ3 be the mean time spent on the new landing page for the different language users - English, French and Spanish respectively.

$H_0: \mu_{1} = \mu_{2} = \mu_{3} $

$H_a$: Atleast one of $\mu_{1}, \mu_{2}$, or $\mu_{3}$ is different from the rest

Test selection¶

  • This is a comparison of means of more than 2 populations
  • The test is concerned with analysis run on two variables 'time_spent_on_the_page' and 'preferred_language' - Bivariate analysis
  • 'time_spent_on_the_page' is a continuous variable and 'language_preferred' is a discrete variable
  • The problem statement indicates 'random selection of users' - random sampling

Hence the most appropriate statistical test is One-way ANOVA f-test

We need to confirm the following 2 conditions for f-test:

  • Shapiro-Wilk's test for normality
  • Levene's test for variance equality

Shapiro-Wilk's test¶

Hypothesis¶

$H_0:$time_spent_on_the_page follows a normal distribution

$H_a:$time_spent_on_the_page does not follow a normal distribution

In [224]:
from scipy.stats import shapiro

stat, pvalue = shapiro(data_newpage['time_spent_on_the_page'])
print('p-value = ', pvalue)

p-value =  0.8040040364746845

The p-value is > (α=0.05)

  • Due to the above, there is NOT enough statistical evidence to reject the null hypothesis.
  • Hence at a 5% level of significance, it is inferred that time_spent_on_the_page follows a normal distribution

Levene's test¶

Hypothesis¶

$H_0:$The variances of all populations for different languages are equal

$H_a:$Atleast one population variance is different

In [225]:
from scipy.stats import levene

pop1 = data_newpage[data_newpage['language_preferred'] == 'English']['time_spent_on_the_page']
pop2 = data_newpage[data_newpage['language_preferred'] == 'Spanish']['time_spent_on_the_page']
pop3 = data_newpage[data_newpage['language_preferred'] == 'French']['time_spent_on_the_page']
stat, pvalue = levene(pop1, pop2, pop3)
print('p-value = ', pvalue)

p-value =  0.46711357711340173

The p-value is > (α=0.05)

  • Due to the above, there is NOT enough statistical evidence to reject the null hypothesis.
  • Hence at a 5% level of significance, it is inferred that the variances of all populations for different languages are equal

Both the conditions for the f_test are satisfied. Hence we can proceed forward with the One-way ANOVA f-test

p-value calculation and comparison with level of significance(α)¶

Inference¶

  • Due to the above, there is NOT enough statistical evidence to reject the null hypothesis
  • Hence at a 5% level of significance, it is inferred that the mean time spent on the new landing page for the different language users is the same

Conclusion and Business Recommendation¶

Conclusions¶

At a (α=0.05) level of significance

  1. Users spend more time on the new landing page than the old landing page
  2. The conversion rate of new landing page is higher than the old landing page
  3. Conversion status is independent of the preferred language
  4. The mean time spent on the new landing page for the different language users is the same

Recommendations¶

  1. E-news Express should introduce the new landing page. Based on statistical evidence, the time spent by users on the new landing page is more than on the old landing page. The conversion rate has also been proved to be higher on the new landing page
  2. As there is no significant evidence of the conversion status with respect to languages, the new landing page can be introduced to all users irrespective of language
  3. E-new Express should try to introduce the new landing page to other language users as well and carry out statistical tests on relationships of attributes with the language
